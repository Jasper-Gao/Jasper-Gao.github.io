Systems of Linear Equations

linear equation: both sides of the equation are a sum of (constant) multiples of x, y, z, …, plus an optional constant


Are not linear

A system of equations is called inconsistent if it has no solutions. It is called consistent otherwise.
all ordered n-tuples of real numbers.这里的tuple指的是数组
An u-tuple of real numbers is called a point of

Examples:
When n=1, the space we got is a number line
When n=2, the space will be a Euclidean plane.

The number tuples can be understood as information carriers. They can either carry a position information on a space, a direction information for a vector, a combination of different elements or some other kinds. The information that a tuple carries is dependent on its application.

A line is a single linear equation in a 2-D plane. A plane is a single linear equation in a 3-D space. More generally, a single linear equation in n variables defines an ”n-1-plane” in n-space.

2 Variables

A solution to the system of both equations is a pair of numbers (x,y) that makes both equations true at once. When two lines do not have solutions, we call the two lines parallel. If the second equation is a multiple of the first, these equations define the same line in the plane and they have infinite solutions.

3 Or More Variables

Two equations for 3 variable space has an intersection in form of a line. In general, the solutions of a system of equations in n variables is the intersection of “(n-1)-planes” in n-space.

Row Reduction

Three operations can be done on equations sets with solution unchanged:

  1. Scaling: we can multiply both sides of an equation by a nonzero number.
  2. Replacement: we can add a multiple of one equation to another, replacing the second equation with the result.
  3. Swap: we can swap two equations.

Augmented Matrices and Row Operations

The matrices that carry coefficient information from equation sets are called augmented matrix. Augmented matrix is used to reduce the duplication of writing variables.
The three rules mentioned above works the same for augmented matrix.

Echelon Forms 梯形式

A matrix is in two echelon form if:

  1. All zero rows are at the bottom
  2. The first nonzero entry of a two is to the right of the first nonzero entry of the row above. 一行的第一个非零数字在上一行第一个非零数字的右下侧。
  3. Below the first nonzero entry of a row, all entries are zero.
    A pivot is the first nonzero entry of a two of a matrix in row echelon form.
    A matrix is in reduced two echelon form if it is in row echelon form and in addition.
  4. Each pivot is equal to 1.
  5. Each pivot is the only nonzero entry in its column.

\right)
